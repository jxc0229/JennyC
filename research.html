QSlack: A slack-variable approach for variational quantum semi-definite programming (pre-print:arXiv:2312.03830)

Authors: Jingxuan (Jenny) Chen, Hanna Westerheim, Zoë Holmes, Ivy Luo, Theshani Nuradha, Dhrumil Patel, Soorya Rethinasamy, Kathie Wang, Mark M. Wilde

Brief description:

In this project, we introduce variational quantum algorithms to solve reformulated semi-definite programming (SDP) problems. Our algorithm aims to utilize the combined computational power of quantum and classical computers to perform optimization tasks beyond the simulation capabilities of classical computers. The algorithm can solve for both the primal and dual of the SDPs to provide a lower and upper bound on the optimal value. It is particularly interesting to me because of its broad applicability–we can apply this algorithm to all problems that can be formulated as SDPs. We applied this algorithm to several problems of interest in quantum information and physics, including estimating trace distance, entanglement negativity, root fidelity, and constrained Hamiltonian optimization.

Dual-VQE: A quantum algorithm to lower bound the ground-state energy (pre-print:arXiv:2312.03083)

Authors: Hanna Westerheim, Jingxuan (Jenny) Chen, Zoë Holmes, Ivy Luo, Theshani Nuradha, Dhrumil Patel, Soorya Rethinasamy, Kathie Wang, Mark M. Wilde

Brief description:

The variational quantum eigensolver (VQE) is a hybrid quantum-classical variational algorithm that produces an upper-bound estimate of the ground-state energy of a Hamiltonian. This project uses the method we introduced in the QSlack paper but only investigates the application of this method in the VQE problems. Our method produces a lower-bound estimate of the ground-state energy. As such, VQE and dual-VQE can serve as quality checks on their solutions; in the ideal case, the VQE upper bound and the dual-VQE lower bound form an interval containing the true optimal value of the ground-state energy. The idea behind dual-VQE is to employ semi-definite programming duality to rewrite the ground-state optimization problem as a constrained maximization problem, which itself can be bounded from below by an unconstrained optimization problem to be solved by a variational quantum algorithm.
